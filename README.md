# sleep-health-lifestyle-analysis

## Dataset
- Dataset used is the Sleep-health-and-lifestyle-dataset retrieved from kaggle
- link: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/uom190346a/sleep-health-and-lifestyle-dataset
- This dataset covers the health and lifestyle of people from varying ages and occupations and checks their sleep quality and duration. Some of the variables that can be analyzed include stress level, BMI category, blood pressure, heart rate, daily steps, and physical activity level. Using these variables, one could find out if a deeper connection lies for one's poor sleep quality or they can see what the group of people who have a high sleep quality are doing to maintain good sleep. 

---
## Questions explored: 
- Does daily steps affect sleep duration?
- Does daily steps affect quality of sleep?
- Is there a relationship between the BMI category and sleep disorders? 
- Does systolic blood pressure have a relationship with sleep disorders?
- Does diastolic blood pressure have a relationship with sleep disorders? 
- Is there a correlation between the BMI category and the quality of sleep? 
- Does systolic blood pressure and quality of sleep correlate?
- Does diastolic blood pressure and quality of sleep correlate?
- Is there a relationship between sleep disorders and quality of sleep?

---
## Key Findings
- Those who fall under the overweight or obese BMI category have worse sleep quality and are more susceptible to sleep disorders
- High blood pressure worsens the quality of sleep and increases the risk of developing a sleep disorder
- Sleep apnea patients show elevated blood pressure
- Daily step count has negligible correlation with both sleep quality and duration
